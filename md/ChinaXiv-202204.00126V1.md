# Abstract

Partitionable sets (PS) and almost partitionable sets (APS) are two important types of combinatorial configurations in design theory. They have intimate connections with other combinatorial structures such as $\mathbb { Z }$ -cyclic patterned starter whist tournaments,cyclic difference matrices, cyclic balanced sampling plans excluding contiguous units,disjoint diference families,and optical orthogonal codes. The existence problems of PS and APS remain far from settled as they demand stringent requirements. This paper focuses on the case of $p \equiv 7$ （20 (mod 8) being a prime and establishes new constructions for partitionable sets of order $p ^ { 2 }$ and almost partitionable sets of order $p$ . In particular,for $p \equiv 7$ (mod 8),this paper shows the existence of PS of order $p ^ { 2 }$ for a large portion of primes $p < \mathsf { 3 0 0 0 0 }$ ， the existence and asymptotic existence of APS under certain conditions,and also the existence of APS of order $p$ for all primes $p < 5 0 0 0 0$ with 16 possible exceptions.
